This project demonstrates a comprehensive data warehousing and analytics solution designed for the retail industry. By leveraging industry-standard tools like SQL Server, Power BI, and Tableau, this solution streamlines sales, customer, and product data into actionable insights to optimize business performance and decision-making.

Project Structure

  • Data Architecture: Built a modern data warehouse following the Medallion Architecture to support reporting and analytics for retail operations.
  • ETL Pipelines: Utilized industry-standard ETL processes to extract, transform, and load data from various systems like ERP and CRM into the warehouse.
  • Data Modeling: Designed star schemas and fact-dimension models for sales, customer, and inventory data.
  • Analytics & Reporting: Delivered real-time insights with Power BI and Tableau dashboards for sales, profit, and customer behavior analysis.

SQL Data Warehouse Overview

  • Data Architecture: This project utilizes a Star Schema structure with fact and dimension tables. We have relationships and foreign keys connecting various entities, with surrogate keys for efficient querying.
  • ETL Process: The data is extracted from CSV files, transformed for consistency and structure, and loaded into SQL tables using BULK INSERT. Data is stored in schema layers, such as the gold schema.

ðŸ“Š Customer Dashboard | Requirements

The customer dashboard aims to provide an overview of customer data, trends, and behaviors. It will help marketing teams and management to understand customer segments and improve customer satisfaction.

Key Requirements:

  • KPI Overview: Display a summary of total number of customers, total sales per customer, and total number of orders for the current year and the previous year.
  • Customer Trends: Present the data for each KPI on a monthly basis for both the current year and the previous year. Identify months with highest and lowest sales and make them easy to recognize.
  • Customer Distribution by Number of Orders: Represent the distribution of customers based on the number of orders they have placed to provide insights into customer behavior, loyalty, and engagement.
  • Top 10 Customers By Profit: Present the top 10 customers who have generated the highest profits for the company, showing additional information like rank, number of orders, current sales, current profit, and the last order date.

ðŸ“Š Sales Dashboard | Requirements

The purpose of the sales dashboard is to present an overview of the sales metrics and trends to analyze year-over-year sales performance and understand sales trends.

Key Requirements:

  • KPI Overview: Display a summary of total sales, profits, and quantity for the current year and the previous year.
  • Sales Trends: Present the data for each KPI on a monthly basis for both the current year and the previous year. Identify months with highest and lowest sales and make them easy to recognize.
  • Product Subcategory Comparison: Compare sales performance by different product subcategories for the current year and the previous year. Include a comparison of sales with profit.
  • Weekly Trends for Sales & Profit: Present weekly sales and profit data for the current year, displaying the average weekly values.
